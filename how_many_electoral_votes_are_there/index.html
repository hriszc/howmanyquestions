How Many Electoral Votes Are There?
A concise breakdown of the 538 votes and why the number matters.
  • There are 538 electoral votes in total. This equals 435 U.S. House seats, 100 Senate seats, plus 3 for Washington, D.C.
  • To win the U.S. presidential election, a candidate must earn at least 270 electoral votes, which is just over half of 538.
  • Each stateâ€™s electoral votes are recalculated after every 10-year census, but the nationwide total has remained 538 since 1964 (after D.C. gained electors).
  • Most states use a winner-take-all rule. Maine and Nebraska use a congressional district method, which your scenario builder can help you approximate.
Tip for quick understanding: If your selected states reach 270 or more in the tool, that combination alone would be enough to secure the presidency under current Electoral College rules.
