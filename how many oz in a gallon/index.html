FAQ: ounces & gallons

Is a gallon always 128 ounces?

128 US fluid ounces applies to the US liquid gallon. The imperial gallon used in the UK and Canada equals 160 UK fluid ounces (which are slightly larger than US ones). The calculator shows both.

How do I convert ounces to gallons quickly?

Divide the number of ounces by 128 to get US gallons. For example, 320 oz ÷ 128 = 2.5 gallons. The input above performs the math automatically and rounds to your preferred precision.

What about cups or liters?

1 gallon = 16 cups and about 3.785 liters. The live summary chips update those equivalents every time you move the slider or type a new value.

Does temperature change the conversion?

Fluid ounces and gallons are volumetric units, so the relationship stays fixed (128:1) for practical kitchen and fuel calculations. Only highly precise laboratory work factors in temperature-driven density changes.
