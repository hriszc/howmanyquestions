How Many Chromosomes Do Humans Have?

Humans typically have 46 chromosomes, arranged in 23 pairs. Use this concise tool to understand what that means, how it splits into sex cells, and quickly answer common exam or quiz questions.

Direct answer
Humans have 46 chromosomes in most body cells.
These are organized into 23 pairs: 22 pairs of autosomes + 1 pair of sex chromosomes (XX or XY).
Total in body cell
46 chromosomes
Diploid (2n) number for humans.
Pairs
23 pairs
Matching sets, one from each parent.
Sex cells
23 chromosomes
Eggs and sperm are haploid (n).

Key fact
The accepted answer to "How many chromosomes do humans have?" is 46 chromosomes in most body cells.
  • 22 pairs of autosomes (44 chromosomes)
  • 1 pair of sex chromosomes (XX or XY)
  • Symbol: 2n = 46, so n = 23
Gametes vs body cells
Human sex cells (eggs and sperm) are haploid (n).
  • Each gamete carries 23 chromosomes (one from each pair).
  • When egg and sperm combine → zygote has 46 chromosomes again.
